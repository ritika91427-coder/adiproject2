About the Department

Multi-Slice CT Scanning Excellence

Our CT Scan Department is equipped with a modern multi-slice CT scanner that captures hundreds of images per rotation, allowing for rapid, high-resolution cross-sectional imaging of the entire body. This technology is essential for emergency diagnosis, cancer staging, vascular assessment, and complex trauma evaluation.

Our board-certified radiologists specialise in interpreting CT findings across all body systems. We offer both plain and contrast-enhanced CT protocols, as well as specialised studies like CT angiography, CT colonoscopy, and low-dose lung CT for cancer screening.

With rapid gantry speed and advanced dose-reduction technology, we deliver exceptional image quality while maintaining the lowest possible radiation exposure for every patient. Emergency CT is available 24×7 for critical care decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

CT Scan FAQs

CT uses low doses of X-ray radiation to create detailed images. Modern scanners use dose-reduction algorithms to keep exposure as low as possible. The diagnostic benefit of a CT scan far outweighs the small radiation risk in most clinical situations. Our radiologists carefully justify each scan and choose the lowest appropriate dose protocol.
Most CT scans take only 5–15 minutes from start to finish. The actual scanning time is often less than 30 seconds. However, preparation (including contrast administration if needed) and post-scan observation may add extra time. Emergency scans are prioritised and completed rapidly.
If contrast dye will be used, you should avoid eating for 4–6 hours before the scan. Plain CT scans generally do not require fasting. Please inform our team of any kidney disease, diabetes, or prior contrast reactions when booking, as these may affect contrast eligibility.
CT in pregnancy requires careful medical justification due to radiation exposure to the foetus. In emergency situations where CT is essential for diagnosis, the procedure is done with shielding when possible. MRI or ultrasound are preferred alternatives during pregnancy for non-emergency imaging.
CT uses X-rays and is faster and better for bone, bleeding, and lung assessment. MRI uses magnets and provides superior soft-tissue detail with no radiation. CT is preferred in emergencies, trauma, and bony structures; MRI is better for brain, spine, joints, and soft tissue. Your doctor will advise the most appropriate study for your condition.
