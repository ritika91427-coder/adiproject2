About the Department

Expert Heart Care Since 1889

The Cardiology Department at Wangduk Health and Research has been at the forefront of cardiac medicine for over a century. Led by Prof. Laslong Wangduk — a nationally recognized cardiologist and Yoshida Award recipient — our team delivers comprehensive heart care from routine check-ups to complex interventional procedures.

Our state-of-the-art cardiac suite is equipped with advanced diagnostic tools including echocardiography, stress testing, Holter monitoring, and modern catheterization facilities. We combine evidence-based medicine with compassionate, patient-centered care to ensure the best outcomes for every patient who walks through our doors.

From preventive cardiology to emergency cardiac interventions, our 24×7 team is always ready. We believe that heart health is the cornerstone of overall well-being, and our multidisciplinary approach ensures that every aspect of your cardiovascular health is addressed with precision and care.

Conditions We Treat

Comprehensive Cardiac Diagnosis

Heart Attack (MI)
Rapid diagnosis and emergency treatment for myocardial infarction to minimize heart muscle damage.
Arrhythmia
Accurate detection and management of irregular heartbeats using advanced Holter and ECG monitoring.
Angina
Effective management of chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle.
Heart Failure
Specialized care for chronic and acute heart failure with personalized treatment plans.
Hypertension
Comprehensive evaluation and long-term management of high blood pressure to prevent complications.
Valve Disease
Diagnosis and treatment of aortic, mitral, and other heart valve disorders.
Coronary Artery Disease
Advanced management of arterial blockages to restore healthy blood flow to the heart.
Congenital Heart Disease
Expert evaluation and treatment of heart conditions present from birth in adults and children.
Treatments & Procedures

Advanced Cardiac Interventions

ECG / Holter Monitoring
12-lead ECG and 24-hour Holter monitoring for accurate detection of rhythm abnormalities.
Echocardiography
High-resolution ultrasound imaging to assess heart structure, function, and valve health.
Angioplasty & Stenting
Minimally invasive procedure to open blocked arteries and restore blood flow.
Cardiac Surgery
Full cardiac surgical services including bypass grafting and valve repair/replacement.
Pacemaker Implantation
Surgical placement of pacemakers to regulate abnormal heart rhythms effectively.
Stress Testing
Exercise and pharmacological stress tests to evaluate cardiac function under workload.

Frequently Asked Questions

Your Cardiology Questions Answered

If you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, palpitations, dizziness, swelling in legs, or persistent fatigue, you should seek cardiac evaluation promptly. Early diagnosis significantly improves outcomes for most heart conditions.
Most ECGs and echocardiograms require no special preparation. Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing. Avoid applying lotions or oils on your chest on the day of the test. For stress tests, avoid caffeine for 24 hours and wear comfortable walking shoes.
Angioplasty is a minimally invasive procedure, not open-heart surgery. It is performed through a small puncture in the wrist or groin. Most patients are discharged within 1–2 days and can resume normal activities within a week.
