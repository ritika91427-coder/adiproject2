About the Department

High-Field MRI Technology

Our MRI unit houses a state-of-the-art high-field MRI scanner, delivering detailed anatomical images with unparalleled clarity. This technology enables physicians to visualise soft tissues, organs, blood vessels, and the nervous system with extraordinary precision — without any radiation exposure.

Our team of experienced radiologists and MRI technologists work together to ensure every scan is conducted with the highest level of patient comfort and clinical accuracy. We offer a wide range of MRI protocols including contrast-enhanced imaging, functional MRI, and specialised joint studies.

Rapid report turnaround means your doctor receives detailed radiological findings quickly, enabling timely treatment decisions. We accept referrals from all specialties and offer emergency MRI access for critical cases.

Frequently Asked Questions

MRI FAQs

MRI is completely safe and uses no ionising radiation. It uses magnetic fields and radio waves to create images. It is safe for most patients including children and pregnant women (after the first trimester with doctor's recommendation). Patients with certain metal implants may not be eligible — our team will screen you beforehand.
Most MRI scans take between 20 and 60 minutes depending on the area being scanned and whether contrast is used. Complex studies like whole-spine or cardiac MRI may take longer. Our team will give you a precise estimate when scheduling.
For most MRI scans, fasting is not required. If a contrast agent will be used, you may be asked to avoid eating for 4–6 hours before the scan. For abdominal MRI, specific preparation instructions will be provided when you book.
Yes. Our team is experienced in managing patients with claustrophobia. We offer anxiolytic medication (sedation) for patients who need it, after physician assessment. Our team will also explain the procedure fully, provide earplugs for noise, and maintain communication throughout the scan.
Routine MRI reports are typically ready within 12–24 hours. Emergency reports are provided within 2 hours. Reports are sent digitally to the referring doctor and are also available at our radiology reception. Detailed images are provided on CD upon request.
